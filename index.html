Mandrake

A mandrake is the root of a plant, historically derived
either from plants of the genus Mandragora found in the
Mediterranean region, or from other species, such as Bryonia
alba, the English mandrake, which have similar properties.
Because mandrakes contain deliriant hallucinogenic tropane
alkaloids and the shape of their roots often resembles human
figures, they have been associated with magic rituals
throughout history, including present day contemporary pagan
traditions such as Wicca and Odinism (Source: Wikipedia).
